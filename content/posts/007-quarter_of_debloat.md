Sometimes I feel the itch to clean up and debloat certain aspects of my life. I'm not a die-hard purist or a minimalist, but this itch became unbearable in the past few months, so I decided to do something about it. There is no better time to do these activities than at the beginning of the new year. So, here is the list I would like to tackle.

<!--more-->
 
# Photos
 
My photo backup strategy before 2015 was rudimental. I had a folder for every year with a subfolder for "significant" events and unsorted pictures. This was stored on the hard drive of my then-Windows-based laptop. As you would expect, the said hard drive failed, rendering photos predating 2005 inaccessible. Moving to Mac in 2015 and subscribing to iCloud storage was a blessing, as this collection is one of my most valuable assets.
 
The library itself is more or less well-organized. Newly taken shots are cherry-picked, so only the best ones are kept. The case is quite the opposite of the old ones, though. There are a lot of near-identical, blurry, and poor-quality ones. Most of them hold sentimental value. It is a curated collection, so the poor ones need to go. Currently, the library has 17 172 photos and 2 215 videos with a cumulative size of 156,5 GB. It will be a hell of an exercise to weed them out.
 
Adding insult to injury, I decided to add location data to the pre-iPhone-era pictures. Of the 17 thousand above 3, 753 do not have GPS data, and 401 of the 22 hundred is also lacking that. Luckily, I have HashPhotos for the rescue, and I scheduled this exercise for the bi-weekly office commute. I'll report on how that went, but it won't be any time soon.
 
# Files, Apps & Services
 
Comically enough, the external hard drive I used in the past years died on me after I moved to my new computer. So, documents and miscellaneous files live on my laptop or some cloud storage, which is far from optimal. This is an opportunity to consolidate this data and move as much as possible to an offline repository. It will have a better structure and will occupy less space. At the same time, I will achieve the goal of moving my stuff off of privacy-invasive services.
 
After the cleansing, I have to develop a better backup strategy and reconsider the choice of applications I use. As a FOSS enthusiast, I will do my best to look for a viable open-source alternative to the software I currently use. I will also revisit the options I have in the cases where open source is not an option. The goal is to use more privacy-respecting and self-sovereign tools without hidden fees. Maybe in a future post, I will go through my stack once it is stabilized.
 
I checked how many entries were in my password manager. The number is too damn high, considering how many web services I use regularly. Most of them are just simple registration for services I was curious about, while the other half is replaceable or duplicate. Gaining traction with the above cleaning projects, it is safe to say that I can weed out this particular part of my digital life.
 
Maybe everything above is a huge pile, but I still plan on shaving it down in Q1.
