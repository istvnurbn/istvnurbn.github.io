I purchased my first proper coffee grinder in 2019 in the form of a Porlex Tall. I solely based my decision on the great video that James Hoffmann did on comparing budget hand grinders at that time. Towards the end of 2022, the taste started to get hollow.

<!--more-->

# gear acquisition syndrome :gear:

Speaking about that video, I started to watch more and more coffee equipment-related content, which resulted in a desire to purchase something nice. First, it was ceramic cups from Sebesi Ceramics, followed by a cutting/serving board from Vinovita.

Remember that part in the introduction where I noted that the taste became hollow? That feeling became more prominent after each visit to a café. I had a nice cup of coffee, purchased the same beans, and prepared with the same method at home, but the taste wasn't as good. So, I went ahead and decided to buy a top-of-the-line hand grinder. The research happened quickly, and I went ahead and ordered a 1Zpresso K-Max unit. After around a month of use, I can say that it was an excellent decision.

![Side by side comparison of the two hand grinders](/images/posts/009/001.jpg "Side by side comparison of the two hand grinders.")

I A/B tested the old and the new grinder with the same coffee and method with one of my friends. We concluded that we would be happier if the difference wouldn't be this stark. It is not a surprise, as the K-MAX's grind size is as uniform as it gets. It produces more fines than I would like it to do so, but I can take that tradeoff any day of the week, as the flavors are excellent. I felt the urge to tell my brother about my finding because he happens to be a food engineer. He made me realize that he had written his thesis on the importance of particle size in powder mixtures. So, it wasn't a surprise for him. Hopefully, my honeymoon with my new grinder will last longer than my memory, as I don't want to invest more in this hobby soon. However, I have some ideas about my upcoming investments... :grinning_face_with_sweat:

# starting a cult :church:

At the end of last year, I took my V60 to the office to prepare a nice cup of coffee after lunch. These items are alien in the kitchen areas of offices where automatic coffee machines prevail. At first, everyone thought that I was the crazy person around here. Since then, months have passed. In those months, I received numerous questions about the coffee and the method as the whole *séance* became an attraction. More curious colleagues asked whether they could have a taste. As usual, this was the first step on a slippery slope.

Fast forward a few months, and this ritual of mine became a base for exploring different types of coffee beans and socializing in the office. Our company has a very relaxed home office policy, so it was hard for many to feel a sense of belonging after things started to get back to normal after the COVID times. I had that feeling too. It fills me with joy that I can contribute in my way of bringing the team together. I'm enjoying coffee more since the whole thing started to develop, and my colleagues enjoy the coffee and the conversations around it. Even my supervisor noted that I have a cult now, as we occupy the main table in the kitchen area.

Giving back to the community helps me have fun in my hobby in a completely different way than before. It pushes me to play my best game and help others to explore new things. It also made me realize that I like it when I have a buzz around me, but being the center of attention still freaks me out.
